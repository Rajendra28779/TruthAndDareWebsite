🎯 Game Overview

This game is a fun and interactive mix inspired by classic games like Ludo, card-based gameplay, and Snakes and Ladders.

It is specially designed for couples (18+) to enjoy playful, romantic, and intimate moments together. The game includes tasks, actions, and challenges that encourage bonding, excitement, and connection.

⚠️ This game is strictly for adults (18+) and may include suggestive content.

🧩 Game Components

  • 48 cards on the track after GO (matches the deck); the last card (highest Sl. No.) is the finish — win by landing there after your turn resolves
  • Mix of dares, picture cards, bonus, and skip tiles
  • 6 bonus cards (+1 / +2 / +3 forward)
  • 5 Block (Skip) cards — Block power & safe-zone rules
  • 🎲 Dice (for movement)

▶️ Gameplay Rules

📌 Rule 1: Starting the Game

Players take turns rolling the dice. The number rolled determines how many steps they move forward on the board.

Exact finish (dice): the last tile on the path (highest Sl. No. on the board) is the finish. Your dice must bring you exactly onto that space. If your roll would jump past it, your pawn does not move and your turn ends.

📌 Rule 2: Card Action

After moving, the player lands on a card:

  • The card is opened
  • A task or action is revealed
  • The player must perform the task

📌 Rule 3: Task Completion

  • ✅ Tap Complete if the task was done → turn ends as normal.
  • ❌ Tap Not complete if they refuse or fail → their pawn moves backward by this turn’s dice roll + 2 (e.g. roll 4 → move back 6), then the turn ends. Stops at GO.
  • 🛡 Or tap Use Block power (if you have stock and haven’t spent one this roll cycle) to skip the task and avoid the penalty — costs 1 Block power.

📌 Rule 4: Bonus Cards

If a player lands on a Bonus Card:

They move forward immediately based on the card:

  • +1 step
  • +2 steps
  • +3 steps

📌 Rule 5: Block (Skip) Cards – Safe Zone

  • If two players are on the same Block (Skip) tile, it is a safe zone.
  • No collision bump — the mover stays and the other is not pushed back.
  • No extra task from that collision; the tile behaves like a normal Skip (turn ends, no card modal).

📌 Rule 6: Block Power (Special Ability)

You earn Block power only when your pawn lands on a Block (Skip) tile from a dice roll and that tile opens for the first time (the “?” reveals). Max 1 charge per player — if you already have 1, opening another Block tile on a later dice landing still leaves you at 1. After you use it (task modal or collision auto-use), stock goes to 0; the next qualifying open sets it back to 1. Bonus slides and collision push-backs do not grant power.

From the task modal you can use it to:

  • Skip the current task
  • Avoid the “Not complete” penalty for that task
  • ✔ You can use it whenever the button appears (you have stock).
  • ✔ Each spend uses one Block power from your stock.
  • ✔ During one full roll-to-resolve cycle (including collision chains), each player can spend at most once; stock carries over — next turn you can spend again if you still have power.

📌 Rule 7: Collision Rule

If the moving player lands on the same tile as the other (not GO, and not a shared Block safe tile — see Rule 5):

  • If Rule 8 applies (bumped player can spend Block power), that runs instead of the push below.
  • Otherwise the mover stays on that tile.
  • The bumped player moves 3 steps backward (stops at GO).
  • The bumped player resolves the new tile first (bonus / skip / card task).
  • Then the mover resolves the tile they landed on.

📌 Rule 8: Block Protection in Collision

If the bumped player has Block power available to spend (stock and not already used this roll cycle — same rule as the task modal):

  • One Block power is automatically consumed.
  • No backward movement.
  • No collision task for the bumped player — the mover resolves their landing tile next.

For a normal card / dare task (not collision), you still choose: Complete, Not complete (penalty), or Use Block power when the button is shown.

📌 Rule 9: Block Card Strategy

  • Stock is 0 or 1 only (same for both players).
  • Only one spend per player per full roll-to-resolve cycle (task modal or collision auto-use).
  • Each spend clears your charge back to 0.
  • Collision: if eligible, protection is automatic. Tasks: Complete, Not complete, or Use Block power.

🏆 Winning the Game

The last card on the track is the finish. You must dice onto it exactly (see Rule 1). When your pawn is on that tile after your turn’s resolution completes (bonuses, tasks, collisions), you win — the win screen shows a fun message for the couple. If both share the finish after resolution, it’s a tie. Use Play again to reset the board with the same names.

However, the main goal is not just winning — it’s about enjoying time together, creating connection, and sharing fun, romantic experiences.
